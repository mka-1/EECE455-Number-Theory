Totient function




Definition:

The totient function φ(n), also called Euler's totient function, is defined as the number of positive integers less or equal to n that are relatively prime to (i.e., do not contain any factor in common with) n, where 1 is counted as being relatively prime to all numbers. Since a number less than or equal to and relatively prime to a given number is called a totative, the totient function φ(n) can be simply defined as the number of totatives of n. For example, there are eight totatives of 24 (1, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17, 19, and 23), so φ(24)=8.
