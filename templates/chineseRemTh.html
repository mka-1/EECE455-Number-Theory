Chinese Remainder Theorem


Definition:

Chinese Remainder Theorem is used to speed up modulo computations. If we're working with a modulo which is a prodcut of numbers; for example: mod M = m1 m2..mk with gcd(mi , mj) = 1. Chinese Remainder theorem lets us work in each moduli mi separately
To see it in action, enter a list of moduli M (ex: 971), and a list of remainders A (ex: 435). The output X in this example will be such that: X ≡ 4 mod 9; X ≡ 3 mod 7; X ≡ 5 mod 1.
